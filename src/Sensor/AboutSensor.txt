


MAX30102 Module: 
    Contains two different types of LED's (Red and IR) and a Photodector 

    Maximum Current Consumption	6mA
    Voltage	3.3-5V 
    Sample Rate	50Hz – 3200Hz
    Temperature Range	 -40°C to +85°C
    Temperature Accuracy	±1˚C
    ADC Resolution	18 bits
    IR LED peak wavelength	880nm
    Red LED peak wavelength	660nm


How does the Pulse Oximeter work? 
    Inside of our blood hemoglobin is responsible for carrying oxygen. 
    When the light from the pulse oximeter passes through our blood, by measering the changes 
    in light absorption we can determine how much oxygen is absorbed in your blood. 
    The sensor contains IR LED and Red LED. Both LED's are used in SpO2 measurments. These two 
    LED's emit different wavelength, IR LED at 880nm, and Red LED at 660nm. The oxygenated and deoxygensated 
    blood have different levels of absorption at these wavelengths.

    Oxygenated blood absorbs more IR light while it reflexts more Red light, while the opposite is true for 
    deoxygensated blood. The reflected light is measured by the Photodector.

    The ration of IR and Red light measured by the Photodector gives us the blood oxygen concentration in the blood 

How does Heart Rate work?

    Then the blood is squeezed in the capliary tissues. The IR sensor can measure the volume of blood moving throught the 
    capliary tissues. using this we can determine heart beat. 
    This is called "Photoplethysmogram"
